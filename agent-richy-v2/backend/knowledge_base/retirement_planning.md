# Retirement Planning

## How Much Do You Need?

### The 25x Rule
Multiply your desired annual retirement spending by 25. This is based on the 4% safe withdrawal rate.
- $40,000/year lifestyle → $1,000,000 needed
- $60,000/year lifestyle → $1,500,000 needed
- $80,000/year lifestyle → $2,000,000 needed
- $100,000/year lifestyle → $2,500,000 needed

### The 4% Rule
You can withdraw 4% of your portfolio in year one of retirement, then adjust for inflation each year. Historically, this has sustained portfolios for 30+ years in 95% of scenarios.

## Retirement Accounts (2025 Limits)

### 401(k)
- **Employee contribution limit:** $23,500/year
- **Catch-up (age 50+):** additional $7,500 ($31,000 total)
- **Super catch-up (ages 60-63):** additional $11,250 ($34,750 total) — new for 2025
- **Total 401(k) limit (including employer):** $70,000
- Traditional 401(k): pre-tax, reduces taxable income now
- Roth 401(k): after-tax, withdrawals are tax-free in retirement

### IRA (Individual Retirement Account)
- **Contribution limit:** $7,000/year ($8,000 if 50+)
- **Roth IRA income limits:** Phase-out begins at $150,000 (single) / $236,000 (married filing jointly)
- **Traditional IRA:** Deductible if no workplace plan or income below limits
- **Backdoor Roth:** High earners can convert traditional IRA contributions to Roth

### HSA (Health Savings Account)
- **2025 limits:** $4,300 (individual) / $8,550 (family)
- Triple tax advantage: deductible going in, grows tax-free, withdrawals tax-free for medical expenses
- After 65, can withdraw for any purpose (taxed like traditional IRA)
- Best strategy: pay medical expenses out of pocket now, let HSA grow, withdraw tax-free later

## Social Security
- Full retirement age: 67 (for those born 1960+)
- Early: age 62 (reduced ~30%)
- Delayed: up to age 70 (increased ~24%)
- Average monthly benefit (2025): ~$1,976
- Do NOT rely on Social Security alone — it's designed to replace only about 40% of pre-retirement income
