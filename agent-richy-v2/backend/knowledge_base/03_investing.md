# Investing Fundamentals & Strategy

## The Investing Roadmap by Age

### Ages 18-25: Foundation Building
- Priority: Emergency fund first ($1,000 minimum, then 3 months expenses)
- Start with: Employer 401(k) up to match (it's FREE money)
- Then: Roth IRA (contribute post-tax, withdraw tax-free in retirement)
- Allocation: 90% stocks / 10% bonds (you have decades to recover from dips)
- Best vehicles: Target-date funds or total market index funds
- Amount: Even $50/month matters. $50/mo at 10% avg return = $379,000 by age 65

### Ages 25-35: Acceleration
- Max out Roth IRA ($7,000/year as of 2025)
- Increase 401(k) contributions toward 15% of income
- Consider taxable brokerage for additional investing
- Start thinking about home purchase (if it makes sense financially)
- Allocation: 80% stocks / 20% bonds

### Ages 35-50: Peak Earning Years
- Max out all tax-advantaged accounts
- Catch-up on retirement if behind (use the "multiply by 25" rule)
- Diversify: real estate, international stocks, bonds
- Allocation: 70% stocks / 30% bonds (adjust based on risk tolerance)

### Ages 50+: Preservation & Income
- Take advantage of catch-up contributions ($7,500 extra for 401k, $1,000 for IRA)
- Shift toward income-producing investments
- Allocation: 50-60% stocks / 40-50% bonds
- Start planning withdrawal strategy

## Index Funds vs Individual Stocks

| Factor | Index Funds | Individual Stocks |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| Diversification | Instant (500+ companies in S&P 500) | You pick, concentrated risk |
| Fees | Very low (0.03% for VTI/VOO) | Trading fees + research time |
| Returns | Market average (~10%/yr historically) | Could beat OR lose to market |
| Time required | Set and forget | Hours of research weekly |
| Success rate | 90% of pros can't beat index funds over 15 years | Very few individuals beat long-term |

**Richy's Take**: "Index funds are boring. And boring is EXACTLY what makes you rich. The S&P 500 has returned ~10% annually over the last 100 years. You don't need to pick winners."

## Roth IRA vs Traditional IRA

| Factor | Roth IRA | Traditional IRA |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| Tax on contributions | You pay taxes NOW | Tax deduction NOW |
| Tax on withdrawals | TAX-FREE in retirement | Taxed as income |
| Income limits (2025) | $161K single / $240K married | No limit (but deduction phases out) |
| Contribution limit | $7,000/yr ($8,000 if 50+) | Same |
| Required distributions | NONE (ever) | Must start at age 73 |
| Best if | You expect higher taxes later | You expect lower taxes later |
| Best for ages | Under 35 (lower tax bracket now) | Over 50 (higher bracket now) |

**The hack**: If you earn too much for a Roth IRA, use a "Backdoor Roth" — contribute to Traditional IRA, then immediately convert to Roth.

## 401(k) Match: The Free Money Guide

If your employer matches 401(k) contributions, ALWAYS contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is a 50-100% instant return on your money.

Example: Employer matches 50% up to 6% of your salary.
- Your salary: $60,000
- You contribute 6%: $3,600/year
- Employer adds 50% match: $1,800/year
- Total going into your 401(k): $5,400/year
- That $1,800 match is FREE. Not contributing enough to get it is literally leaving money on the table.

## Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA)

Invest a fixed amount at regular intervals regardless of market price.

Why it works:
- When prices are HIGH, your fixed amount buys fewer shares
- When prices are LOW, your fixed amount buys MORE shares
- Over time, this averages out your cost per share
- Removes emotion from investing (no trying to "time the market")

Historical proof: An investor who put $500/month into the S&P 500 from 2000-2020 (through TWO major crashes) would have invested $120,000 and ended with approximately $345,000.

## Crypto: What You Actually Need to Know

1. Crypto is speculative, NOT investing in the traditional sense
2. Never invest more than you can afford to lose completely
3. Bitcoin and Ethereum are the most established — everything else is higher risk
4. Dollar cost average if you want exposure (don't lump sum)
5. Keep crypto to 5% or less of your total portfolio
6. Use a hardware wallet for significant holdings
7. Understand: past returns don't guarantee future returns
8. Tax implications: every sale is a taxable event

**Richy's Honest Take**: "I won't tell you to avoid crypto. But I WILL tell you to max out your 401(k) match, build your emergency fund, and pay off high-interest debt FIRST. Then, if you want to put 5% into crypto with money you'd be okay losing? Go for it."

## Tax-Loss Harvesting

Selling investments at a loss to offset capital gains taxes.

How it works:
1. Sell an investment that's down (realizing the loss)
2. Use that loss to offset gains from other investments
3. Can offset up to $3,000 of ordinary income per year
4. Excess losses carry forward to future years
5. Buy a SIMILAR (but not identical) investment to maintain your position

**Important**: The wash-sale rule prevents you from buying the "substantially identical" security within 30 days. Buy something similar but not the same (e.g., sell VTI, buy ITOT).
