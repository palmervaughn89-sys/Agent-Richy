# Budgeting & Cash Flow Mastery

## The 50/30/20 Rule

The simplest budgeting framework that actually works. Split your after-tax income:
- **50% Needs**: Rent/mortgage, utilities, groceries, insurance, minimum debt payments, transportation
- **30% Wants**: Dining out, entertainment, subscriptions, shopping, hobbies
- **20% Savings & Debt**: Emergency fund, investments, extra debt payments

### By Income Level

## Zero-Based Budgeting

Every dollar gets a job. Income minus ALL assigned categories = $0.

Steps:
1. Write your total monthly income at the top
2. List every expense category (rent, food, gas, etc.)
3. Assign a dollar amount to each until you hit $0
4. Track actual spending against each category
5. Adjust monthly based on what actually happened

Best for: People who want maximum control and are willing to track closely.

## Envelope Budgeting (Digital Version)

The old-school method, modernized. Create virtual "envelopes" for variable spending:
- Groceries: $400/mo
- Dining out: $150/mo
- Gas: $200/mo
- Fun money: $100/mo

When an envelope is empty, you stop spending in that category until next month. Apps like YNAB, Goodbudget, and EveryDollar automate this.

## Subscription Audit Checklist

Average American spends $219/month on subscriptions. Run this audit:
- [ ] List every recurring charge (check bank statements for last 3 months)
- [ ] Mark each: ESSENTIAL / NICE-TO-HAVE / FORGOT I HAD THIS
- [ ] Cancel everything in the "forgot" category immediately
- [ ] Negotiate or downgrade "nice-to-have" items
- [ ] Check for free alternatives (library apps for audiobooks, free Spotify tier, etc.)

Common hidden subscriptions: cloud storage, old streaming trials, gym memberships, app subscriptions, insurance add-ons, premium app tiers.

## Paycheck-to-Paycheck Escape Plan

### 30-Day Sprint
- Track every dollar spent (use an app or notebook)
- Identify your top 3 "money leaks"
- Cut one unnecessary expense completely
- Set up auto-transfer of $25/paycheck to savings (even if it feels small)

### 60-Day Milestone
- Build a $500 starter emergency fund
- Negotiate one bill (call and ask for a lower rate)
- Start the debt snowball on your smallest debt
- Increase auto-savings to $50/paycheck

### 90-Day Transformation
- Emergency fund hits $1,000
- One debt fully paid off (or close)
- Subscription costs reduced by 30%+
- You now have a working budget you actually follow

## Monthly Bill Calendar Strategy

Align your bills with your pay schedule to avoid cash crunches:

**If paid bi-weekly**: Split bills into "1st paycheck" and "2nd paycheck" groups. Put rent/mortgage on the larger paycheck. Spread utilities across both.

**If paid monthly**: Pay all bills within the first 5 days. What's left is your true spending money. No surprises.

**Richy's Pro Tip**: "Set up autopay for everything that has a fixed amount. For variable bills (electricity, water), set up autopay for the estimated average. You'll never miss a payment again, and your credit score will thank you."
