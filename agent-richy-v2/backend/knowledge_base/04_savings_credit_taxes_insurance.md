# Savings & Emergency Funds

## Emergency Fund Calculator
Target = Monthly Essential Expenses × Months of Coverage

| Job Stability | Recommended Coverage |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Government/tenured | 3 months |
| Stable full-time job | 3-6 months |
| Freelance/gig/commission | 6-9 months |
| Single income household | 6-9 months |
| Self-employed | 9-12 months |

Essential expenses include: rent/mortgage, utilities, food, insurance, transportation, minimum debt payments. Do NOT include wants like dining out or entertainment.

## Where to Keep Your Emergency Fund
- High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA): Currently 4-5% APY at online banks
- Money Market Account: Similar rates, sometimes with check-writing
- NOT in stocks, crypto, or CDs (need instant access)
- NOT under your mattress (inflation eats it)

Top HYSA options to research: Marcus (Goldman Sachs), Ally Bank, Capital One 360, Discover, Wealthfront Cash

## Sinking Funds
Save for predictable large expenses by setting aside money monthly:
- Car repairs: $100/mo → $1,200/year ready when something breaks
- Holiday gifts: $50/mo → $600 by December
- Vacation: $150/mo → $1,800 for a nice trip
- Car replacement: $200/mo → building toward next down payment

## CD Laddering Strategy
Split savings across CDs with staggered maturity dates:
- $2,000 in a 3-month CD
- $2,000 in a 6-month CD
- $2,000 in a 12-month CD
- $2,000 in a 18-month CD

As each matures, reinvest into a new 18-month CD. You get higher rates while maintaining regular access to portions of your money.

# Credit & Credit Scores

## How Credit Scores Work

### FICO Score Breakdown
| Factor | Weight | How to Optimize |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Payment History | 35% | Never miss a payment. Set up autopay. |
| Credit Utilization | 30% | Keep balances below 30% of limit (10% is ideal) |
| Length of History | 15% | Don't close old cards. Age matters. |
| Credit Mix | 10% | Having different types (cards, loans, mortgage) helps |
| New Inquiries | 10% | Don't apply for multiple cards at once |

### Score Ranges
| Range | Rating | What You Get |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| 800-850 | Exceptional | Best rates on everything |
| 740-799 | Very Good | Excellent rates |
| 670-739 | Good | Decent rates, most approvals |
| 580-669 | Fair | Higher rates, some denials |
| Under 580 | Poor | Difficulty getting approved |

## Building Credit from Zero
1. Get a secured credit card ($200-500 deposit)
2. Use it for ONE small recurring charge (Netflix, gas)
3. Set up autopay for full balance
4. After 6-12 months, request upgrade to unsecured card
5. Alternative: Become an authorized user on a family member's card (their good history helps you)
6. Credit builder loans (Self, MoneyLion) report payments to bureaus

## Credit Repair: Legit Steps
1. Get free credit reports (AnnualCreditReport.com)
2. Dispute any errors in writing (30-day response required)
3. Pay down high-utilization cards first
4. Negotiate "pay for delete" on collections
5. DO NOT pay credit repair companies (you can do everything they do for free)
6. Time heals: most negatives fall off after 7 years, bankruptcies after 10

# Tax Essentials

## Tax Brackets Explained (It's Not What You Think)
Common misconception: "If I earn more, ALL my income gets taxed at the higher rate."
Reality: Only the income WITHIN each bracket is taxed at that rate.

Example (2025 Single Filer):
- First $11,600 → taxed at 10% = $1,160
- $11,601 - $47,150 → taxed at 12% = $4,266
- $47,151 - $100,525 → taxed at 22% = (whatever's in this bracket)

If you earn $60,000, your EFFECTIVE tax rate is about 14%, not 22%.

## Commonly Missed Deductions
- Student loan interest (up to $2,500)
- Home office deduction (if self-employed)
- Health Savings Account (HSA) contributions
- Charitable donations (even without itemizing, up to $300)
- State and local taxes (SALT, up to $10,000)
- Educator expenses (teachers get $300)
- Moving expenses (military only now)
- Energy-efficient home improvements (tax credits)

## Tax-Advantaged Accounts Cheat Sheet
| Account | Tax Benefit | 2025 Limit | Best For |
|---------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| 401(k) | Pre-tax contributions | $23,500 | Retirement, employer match |
| Roth IRA | Tax-free withdrawals | $7,000 | Retirement, young earners |
| HSA | Triple tax advantage | $4,300 single / $8,550 family | Healthcare + stealth retirement |
| 529 Plan | Tax-free growth for education | Varies by state | Saving for kids' college |
| FSA | Pre-tax for healthcare | $3,300 | Predictable medical expenses |

The HSA is the most powerful account in the tax code: deduction going in, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawals for medical expenses. After 65, it works like a Traditional IRA for any purpose.

# Insurance Essentials

## Insurance You Need by Life Stage

### Single, No Dependents (20s)
- Health insurance (mandatory)
- Renters insurance (~$15/month, covers your stuff)
- Auto insurance (required by law)
- Skip: life insurance (no one depends on your income)

### Married / Partner
- Add: Life insurance (term, 10-12x your income)
- Add: Disability insurance (protects your earning power)
- Upgrade: Auto if sharing vehicles

### With Kids
- Increase: Life insurance (both parents)
- Add: Umbrella policy ($1M+ liability protection, ~$200/year)
- Consider: 529 plan (education savings)

### Homeowner
- Homeowners insurance (required by lender)
- Flood insurance (if in flood zone, NOT covered by standard homeowners)
- Umbrella policy (definitely now)

## Term vs Whole Life Insurance
| Factor | Term Life | Whole Life |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Cost | $20-50/month | $200-500/month |
| Coverage period | 10-30 years | Lifetime |
| Cash value | None | Builds slowly |
| Best for | 99% of people | Almost nobody (see below) |
| ROI | Pure insurance, cheap | Terrible investment vehicle |

**Richy's Take**: "Buy term life insurance and invest the difference. A $500K 20-year term policy costs about $30/month for a healthy 30-year-old. Whole life for the same coverage would be $400+/month. Put that $370 difference in an index fund instead."
