# Emergency Funds

## What Is an Emergency Fund?
Cash reserves set aside for unexpected expenses — job loss, medical bills, car repairs, home emergencies. This is your financial safety net.

## How Much Do You Need?

### The Standard Rule: 3-6 Months of Essential Expenses
Essential expenses include: rent/mortgage, groceries, utilities, insurance, transportation, minimum debt payments.

### Your Number Depends On:
- **3 months** if: Dual income household, stable job, no dependents
- **6 months** if: Single income, variable income, have dependents, self-employed
- **9-12 months** if: Freelancer, seasonal worker, commission-based income, single parent

### Quick Calculation
Monthly essentials × months of coverage = emergency fund target.
Example: $3,000/month × 6 months = $18,000 target.

## Building Your Emergency Fund

### Starting from Zero
1. **First milestone: $1,000** — Covers most small emergencies (car repair, medical copay, appliance replacement)
2. **Second milestone: 1 month of expenses** — Buys time if income drops
3. **Third milestone: 3 months** — Handles job loss in strong job market
4. **Final target: 6 months** — Full financial security

### Strategies to Build Faster
- **Automate first:** Set up auto-transfer on payday, even $25/week
- **Sell unused items:** Declutter and sell on Facebook Marketplace, eBay
- **Side hustle:** Dedicate 100% of side income to emergency fund until funded
- **Tax refund:** Direct your refund straight to savings
- **Round up:** Use apps like Acorns or bank round-up features

## Where to Keep Your Emergency Fund
- **High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA):** Best option. Earns 4-5% APY (2025), FDIC insured, accessible in 1-2 days.
- **Money Market Account:** Similar yields, may come with check-writing ability.
- **NOT stocks or investments:** Emergency fund must be stable and liquid. Markets can drop 30%+ when you need the money most (recessions often cause both job loss AND market crashes).
- **NOT your checking account:** Too tempting to spend. Keep it separate.

## When to Use Your Emergency Fund
**YES:** Job loss, medical emergency, essential car/home repair, urgent travel for family emergency.
**NO:** Sales, vacations, "good deals," planned expenses, wants.

## After Using Your Emergency Fund
Replenish it as your #1 financial priority before resuming extra investing or discretionary spending.

## Emergency Fund vs. Other Savings
| Type | Purpose | Where to Keep | How Much |
|---|---|---|---|
| Emergency Fund | Unexpected crises | HYSA | 3-6 months expenses |
| Sinking Fund | Planned irregular expenses | HYSA | Varies by goal |
| Investment Account | Long-term wealth | Brokerage | As much as possible |
