# Budgeting Methods

## The 50/30/20 Rule
Split after-tax (take-home) income into three categories:
- **50% Needs:** Housing, groceries, utilities, insurance, minimum debt payments, transportation
- **30% Wants:** Dining out, entertainment, subscriptions, shopping, travel, hobbies
- **20% Savings & Debt Payoff:** Emergency fund, retirement contributions, extra debt payments, investments

### Example on $5,000/month take-home:
- Needs: $2,500
- Wants: $1,500
- Savings: $1,000

If your needs exceed 50%, focus on reducing your largest fixed expenses (housing and transportation).

## Zero-Based Budget
Every dollar gets assigned a job. Income minus all categories equals exactly zero.
- Best for people who want detailed control
- Can use apps like YNAB (You Need A Budget) or EveryDollar

## Pay Yourself First
1. Set up automatic transfers on payday to savings and investment accounts
2. Pay all bills
3. Whatever is left is your spending money
- Best for people who hate tracking every purchase

## Envelope Method
- Withdraw cash for variable spending categories
- Put cash in physical (or digital) envelopes: Groceries, Dining, Entertainment, etc.
- When an envelope is empty, stop spending in that category
- Best for overspenders who need tangible limits

## Common Budget Mistakes
1. **Not tracking subscriptions** — Average American spends $219/month on subscriptions
2. **Forgetting irregular expenses** — Car maintenance, gifts, annual fees
3. **Being too restrictive** — Budgets without fun money fail. Include a guilt-free category.
4. **Not adjusting** — Review and adjust monthly for the first 3 months

## Budget Percentages by Category (Recommended)
- Housing: 25-30%
- Transportation: 10-15%
- Food (groceries + dining): 10-15%
- Insurance: 10-15%
- Savings/Investments: 15-20%
- Utilities: 5-10%
- Entertainment: 5-10%
- Personal/Misc: 5-10%
