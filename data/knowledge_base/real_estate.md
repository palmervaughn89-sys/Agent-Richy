# Real Estate and Mortgages

## Home Buying Basics

### The 28/36 Rule
- **28% rule:** Monthly housing costs (mortgage + tax + insurance) should be ≤ 28% of gross monthly income
- **36% rule:** Total debt payments should be ≤ 36% of gross monthly income
- Example on $100K salary ($8,333/month): Max housing = $2,333, max total debt = $3,000

### Down Payment
- **Conventional loan:** 5-20% down. Under 20% requires PMI ($100-300/month on $300K home)
- **FHA loan:** 3.5% down (lower credit score OK, 580+)
- **VA loan:** 0% down (veterans/active military)
- **USDA loan:** 0% down (rural areas, income limits)
- **Saving 20% down on $350K home = $70,000** — takes average buyer 5-7 years

### Current Mortgage Rates (2025)
- 30-year fixed: approximately 6.5-7.5%
- 15-year fixed: approximately 5.5-6.5%
- ARM (adjustable): Lower initial rate, adjusts after 5-7 years

### Closing Costs
- Typically 2-5% of purchase price
- On $350K home: $7,000-17,500
- Includes: appraisal, title insurance, attorney fees, origination fees, prepaid taxes and insurance

## Mortgage Math

### Monthly Payment (30-Year Fixed)
At 7% rate:
| Home Price | Down 20% | Monthly P&I | Total Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| $250,000 | $50,000 | $1,331 | $479,017 |
| $350,000 | $70,000 | $1,863 | $670,824 |
| $450,000 | $90,000 | $2,395 | $862,631 |
| $550,000 | $110,000 | $2,928 | $1,054,437 |

Add 30-40% for taxes, insurance, maintenance for true monthly cost.

### 15-Year vs 30-Year
$300K loan at 7% (30-year) vs 6.5% (15-year):
- 30-year: $1,996/month, $418,527 total interest
- 15-year: $2,613/month, $170,362 total interest
- **Savings: $248,165** — but $617/month more

### Refinancing
Worth considering when you can drop rate by 0.75-1%+ and plan to stay 3+ years. Break-even: closing costs ÷ monthly savings = months to recoup.

## Rent vs Buy Decision

### Price-to-Rent Ratio
Home price ÷ annual rent
- Under 15: Buying favored
- 15-20: Roughly equal
- Over 20: Renting may be better

### The 5-Year Rule
Plan to stay at least 5 years to make buying worthwhile after transaction costs (buying costs + selling costs = ~10% of home value).

### Hidden Costs of Ownership
- Property taxes: 0.5-2.5% of home value annually
- Maintenance: 1-2% of home value annually
- Homeowners insurance: $1,500-3,000/year
- HOA fees: $0-$500+/month
- Capital improvements: New roof ($10-25K), HVAC ($5-10K), water heater ($1-3K)

## Real Estate as Investment

### Primary Residence
- Builds equity over time
- Tax benefits: Mortgage interest deduction (if itemizing), up to $250K ($500K married) capital gains exclusion
- Historical appreciation: ~3-5% annually (varies widely by area)
- NOT purely an investment — it's a place to live that may appreciate

### Rental Properties
- Cap rate (net operating income ÷ purchase price) should be > 5%
- Budget for vacancies (8-10% of rental income), maintenance, property management
- Leverage amplifies returns but also risk
- Not passive income without a property manager

## First-Time Buyer Tips
1. Get pre-approved BEFORE house hunting
2. Don't skip the home inspection ($300-500, can save you $50,000+)
3. Budget for unexpected repairs (set aside 1% of home value/year day one)
4. Don't buy the most expensive home you qualify for — that's the bank's max, not yours
5. Consider total commute cost — a cheaper house 30 miles out may cost more in gas and time
