# Credit Scores and Reports

## Understanding Your Credit Score

### FICO Score Ranges (300-850)
| Score | Rating | Population % | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| 800-850 | Exceptional | ~21% | Best rates on everything |
| 740-799 | Very Good | ~25% | Near-best rates |
| 670-739 | Good | ~21% | Average rates |
| 580-669 | Fair | ~17% | Subprime rates, higher costs |
| 300-579 | Poor | ~16% | May be denied credit |

### The 5 Factors
1. **Payment History (35%)** — Most important. Even one 30-day late payment can drop your score 60-110 points. Set up autopay for minimums on everything.
2. **Credit Utilization (30%)** — Balance ÷ credit limit. Keep under 30%, ideally under 10%. Applies to each card AND overall. Pay before statement closing date to lower reported utilization.
3. **Length of Credit History (15%)** — Average age of all accounts. Never close your oldest credit card. If you have no credit history, become an authorized user on a family member's old card.
4. **Credit Mix (10%)** — Having different types (credit card, auto loan, mortgage, student loan) helps slightly.
5. **New Inquiries (10%)** — Each hard inquiry costs 5-10 points temporarily. Multiple inquiries for same type (mortgage shopping) within 14-45 days count as one.

## Building Credit from Scratch

### Step 1: Get Your First Credit Card
- Secured credit card (requires deposit): Discover It Secured, Capital One Platinum Secured
- Student credit card if in college
- Become authorized user on parent's/spouse's card with good history

### Step 2: Use It Responsibly
- Use for ONE small recurring purchase (gas, streaming subscription)
- Pay balance in FULL every month (before the due date)
- Never use more than 30% of your limit

### Step 3: Wait and Build
- After 6 months, you'll have a scoreable credit file
- After 12-24 months, apply for a better card

## Quick Ways to Improve Your Score
1. **Pay down credit cards** to under 30% utilization (fastest impact: 1-2 months)
2. **Get added as authorized user** on someone's old, low-utilization card
3. **Dispute errors** on credit report (30% of reports contain errors)
4. **Request credit limit increase** (lowers utilization ratio without paying anything)
5. **Keep old accounts open** even if you don't use them
6. **Use Experian Boost** (free, adds utility/phone payment history)

## Checking Your Credit
- **Free credit reports:** AnnualCreditReport.com (free weekly from all 3 bureaus)
- **Free credit scores:** Credit Karma, Discover, many bank apps
- Checking your own credit is a "soft inquiry" — it never hurts your score

## The Cost of Bad Credit
On a $300,000 30-year mortgage:
- 6.5% rate (excellent credit): $1,896/month — $382,633 total interest
- 8.0% rate (poor credit): $2,201/month — $492,467 total interest
- **Difference: $109,834** more in interest over the life of the loan

## Protecting Your Credit
- Freeze credit at all 3 bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) — free, prevents unauthorized accounts
- Monitor for suspicious activity monthly
- Never share credit card numbers over unsecured channels
