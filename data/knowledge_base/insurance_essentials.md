# Insurance Essentials

## Why Insurance Matters
Insurance protects you from financial catastrophe. The rule: insure against things that would **financially devastate** you. Don't insure small, affordable losses.

## Types of Insurance Everyone Needs

### Health Insurance
- **Average premium (2025):** $477/month individual (employer-sponsored: ~$138 after employer contribution)
- **Key terms:** Premium (monthly cost), deductible (you pay first), copay (fixed per visit), out-of-pocket max (your annual ceiling)
- **HSA-eligible plans (HDHP):** Higher deductible, lower premium, can contribute to HSA for triple tax benefit
- **ACA Marketplace:** Open enrollment Nov 1 – Jan 15. Subsidies available based on income.

### Auto Insurance
- **Required:** Liability coverage (minimum varies by state)
- **Recommended:** Comprehensive + collision if car worth > $10,000
- **Ways to save:** Raise deductible to $1,000, bundle with home insurance, maintain good credit, shop around yearly
- **Average cost (2025):** $2,150/year for full coverage

### Renters Insurance
- **Average cost:** $15-25/month
- **Covers:** Personal belongings (theft, fire, water damage), liability if someone is injured in your apartment
- **Often overlooked** but incredibly valuable. Your landlord's insurance does NOT cover your stuff.

### Homeowners Insurance
- **Average cost (2025):** $2,100/year
- **Covers:** Structure, personal property, liability, additional living expenses
- **Tips:** Review annually, increase coverage as home value rises, document belongings with photos/video
- **Not covered:** Floods (need separate NFIP policy), earthquakes (separate policy in affected areas)

### Life Insurance
- **Who needs it:** Anyone with dependents (spouse, kids, aging parents you support)
- **Type to buy:** Term life insurance (20-30 year term, pure coverage, cheap)
- **How much:** 10-15× your annual income
- **Example:** 30-year-old, healthy, $500K 20-year term: ~$25-35/month
- **Skip:** Whole life insurance (expensive, bad investment returns — invest the savings difference instead)

### Disability Insurance
- **Most overlooked insurance.** You're more likely to become disabled than die before 65.
- **Short-term disability:** Covers 60-70% of income for 3-6 months
- **Long-term disability:** Covers 50-60% of income for years/decades
- **Check your employer benefits first** — many include basic coverage

### Umbrella Insurance
- **Extra liability coverage** beyond auto and home limits
- **Who needs it:** Net worth over $300,000, rental property owners, anyone at risk of lawsuit
- **Cost:** ~$150-300/year for $1 million coverage — incredible value

## Insurance Rules of Thumb
1. **Raise deductibles** to lower premiums (but keep enough cash for the deductible)
2. **Bundle policies** for 10-25% discount
3. **Shop around every 2-3 years** — loyalty penalties are real
4. **Never insure what you can afford to replace** (skip extended warranties, phone insurance)
5. **Review coverage annually** as life changes (marriage, kids, home purchase)

## Insurance Scams to Avoid
- Whole life insurance sold as an "investment"
- Credit card protection insurance
- Extended warranties on electronics
- Single-disease policies (cancer insurance, etc.)
- Mortgage life insurance (get term life instead, it's cheaper and more flexible)
