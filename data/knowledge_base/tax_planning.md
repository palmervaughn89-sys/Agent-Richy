# Tax Planning Basics

## 2025 Federal Income Tax Brackets (Single Filers)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 – $11,925 | 10% |
| $11,926 – $48,475 | 12% |
| $48,476 – $103,350 | 22% |
| $103,351 – $197,300 | 24% |
| $197,301 – $250,525 | 32% |
| $250,526 – $626,350 | 35% |
| $626,351+ | 37% |

## 2025 Standard Deduction
- Single: $15,000
- Married Filing Jointly: $30,000
- Head of Household: $22,500
- Age 65+ additional: $2,000 (single) / $1,600 (married)

## Key Tax-Saving Strategies

### 1. Maximize Pre-Tax Retirement Contributions
- 401(k): $23,500 contribution = ~$5,875 tax savings (in 25% bracket)
- Traditional IRA: $7,000 deduction if eligible
- HSA: $4,300 individual / $8,550 family

### 2. Tax-Loss Harvesting
Sell investments at a loss to offset gains. Net losses up to $3,000/year can offset ordinary income. Unused losses carry forward forever.

### 3. Capital Gains Tax Rates (2025)
- Short-term (held < 1 year): Taxed as ordinary income
- Long-term (held > 1 year): 0% if income under $47,025 (single), 15% up to $518,900, 20% above

### 4. HSA Triple Tax Advantage
- Contributions are tax-deductible
- Investments grow tax-free
- Withdrawals for qualified medical expenses are tax-free
- After 65, HSA becomes like a traditional IRA for any purpose

### 5. Charitable Giving Strategies
- Donate appreciated stock (skip capital gains, deduct full market value)
- Donor-advised fund: Front-load contributions for big deduction year
- Qualified Charitable Distribution from IRA (age 70½+)

### 6. Roth Conversions
Convert traditional IRA/401(k) to Roth in low-income years. Pay tax now, never again. Especially valuable in sabbatical years, early retirement, or between jobs.

## Common Tax Mistakes
1. Not adjusting withholding (getting huge refunds = free loan to government)
2. Missing the April 15 deadline (or extension date October 15)
3. Not keeping receipts for deductible expenses
4. Forgetting state taxes when planning
5. Not contributing to tax-advantaged accounts

## Self-Employment Taxes
- Self-employment tax: 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare) on first $176,100
- Above $176,100: only 2.9% Medicare
- Deductions: Home office, mileage ($0.70/mile in 2025), health insurance, retirement contributions (SEP IRA up to $70,000)

*Note: Tax laws change frequently. Consult a CPA or tax professional for your specific situation.*
